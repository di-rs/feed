A deep analysis of the disconnect between AI academic breakthroughs and industrial deployment failures, revealing that 95% of generative AI pilots fall short while research like AlphaFold wins Nobel Prizes. The article argues that the entire AI development enterprise has been optimized for the wrong outcomes—publication citations and revenue rather than solving societally important problems—and proposes ethical problem framing as the path forward.
