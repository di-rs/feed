Good documentation saves time, improves team collaboration, helps new developers onboard faster, and demonstrates professionalism. Documentation should be simple, clear, updated, and beginner-friendly, covering product (what & why), technical (how), and process (who & when) aspects. While code builds the product, documentation builds the team and serves as a single source of truth for the entire project.
