In Power BI, primary keys uniquely identify records in dimension tables, while foreign keys (secondary keys) in fact tables link back to those dimension tables to form a complete data model. Understanding this relationship is foundational to effective data modeling in Power BI, which works entirely with structured tabular data. The article uses clear analogies — primary key as a nametag, foreign key as a pointer — to make the concepts accessible for beginners.
