Every decade brings a new boogeyman to kill QA — first Agile, then automation, then shift-left, now AI — but the role has survived and evolved each time. History shows that companies like Google and Microsoft simply transformed QA into engineering-focused roles (Test Engineers, SWETs) rather than eliminating quality practices altogether. The testers who adapt by understanding systems, edge cases, and risk remain indispensable regardless of tooling shifts.
