An honest essay naming "AI fatigue" — the cognitive overload of managing 4+ AI tools daily, each with different prompting styles, interfaces, and mental models that create compounding switching costs. The author identifies three distinct types: tool fatigue (subscription overload), prompt fatigue (the mental tax of talking to literal machines), and output fatigue (everything sounding like the same over-polished intern). The proposed fix is a strict "two-tool rule" that forces brutal prioritization over FOMO-driven tool hopping.
