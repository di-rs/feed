A software engineer reflects on how AI tools — from GitHub Copilot to Claude — have dramatically reshaped their workflow over the past few years, boosting output on mundane tasks but raising deeper questions about the nature of their work. The tension lies in AI's promise of automating exactly the kind of problem-solving developers used to get paid for, prompting a rethink of what skills and judgment remain uniquely human. The piece is a candid look at the push toward efficiency and the pull of professional identity in an AI-augmented industry.
