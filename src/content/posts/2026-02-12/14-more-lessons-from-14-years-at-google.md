Addy Osmani shares 14 additional lessons from his tenure at Google, this time focused on team dynamics and organizational effectiveness rather than individual craft. Key insights include: ruthlessly prioritizing which problems deserve your attention, always naming the specific decision needed before calling a meeting (approve/choose/unblock/inform), and understanding how decisions actually get made versus how they appear to be made. The post is a follow-up to his widely-read "21 lessons" and skews toward the systems and coordination challenges that separate shipping teams from spinning ones.
