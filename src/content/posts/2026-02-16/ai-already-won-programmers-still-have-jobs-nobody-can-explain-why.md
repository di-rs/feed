AI is genuinely threatening traditional programming roles — junior devs writing CRUD endpoints and mid-level devs implementing well-defined tickets are most at risk. However, the software industry itself remains safe because most people lack the willingness to use AI tools themselves, preferring to pay someone else (the "$10 Fiverr problem"). The real bottleneck was never tool access but cognitive effort — people don't want to build software, they want software built for them.
