AI tools now generate boilerplate code, unit tests, and complex logic, directly impacting entry-level developer roles and allowing smaller teams to ship faster. However, AI still struggles with architectural trade-offs, ambiguous requirements, and business-critical decisions — areas where experienced developers remain essential. The future developer role is shifting from code writer to "software orchestrator" who designs systems, guides AI tools, and owns outcomes.
