WordPress still powers 43% of the web, and while developers often cite modern alternatives like Next.js and headless CMS as "WordPress killers," they actually solve different problems. Most performance complaints stem from poor architecture—excessive plugins, unoptimized themes, and shared hosting—rather than WordPress itself. The platform remains technically strong for content management at scale, SEO infrastructure, and REST API-driven headless setups.
