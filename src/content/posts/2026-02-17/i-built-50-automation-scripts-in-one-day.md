A developer built 50 automation scripts in 18 hours for their side project pipeline — health checks, publishers, scrapers — only to realize they had automated a business with zero revenue and zero traffic. The core lesson: automating before validating is a trap; you end up optimizing a process nobody needs instead of solving the underlying problem. Only 3 of the 50 scripts turned out to be genuinely useful, proving that efficiency without product-market fit is just busywork at scale.
