At the 62nd Munich Security Conference, Google's Kent Walker highlighted how AI-powered defenders gain a decisive edge against adversaries who are already using AI for automated reconnaissance and hyper-realistic phishing. The conference underscored that isolated security tools and fragmented defenses are insufficient against multi-front threats, calling for a full-stack, collaborative approach. Google also warned that cryptographically relevant quantum computers are closer than expected, urging organizations to begin migrating to post-quantum cryptography now.
