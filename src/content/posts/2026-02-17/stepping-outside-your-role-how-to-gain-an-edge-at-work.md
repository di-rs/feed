A frontend developer reflects on how a project entering maintenance mode became an opportunity to broaden skills by writing automated tests, configuring Kibana dashboards, building performance dashboards, and even working with Java Spring Boot backend code. The key insight is that in most teams, being the engineer who can cover multiple areas and "gets things done" is more valuable than narrow specialization. Taking on tasks nobody else wants to do is how you accelerate learning while getting paid for it.
