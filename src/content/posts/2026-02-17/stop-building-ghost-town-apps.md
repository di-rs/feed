Most recipe app side projects fail because they're just CRUD apps that ignore the real user problem: decision fatigue around "what do I eat tonight?" The author argues that a "Tinder for Food" swipe model outperforms choice-heavy UIs by 4x in session length, and that the real monetization opportunity lies in grocery API referral engines (Instacart et al.) rather than recipe content itself. Adding a photo-to-recipe vision feature is now considered the primary hook for Gen Z retention.
