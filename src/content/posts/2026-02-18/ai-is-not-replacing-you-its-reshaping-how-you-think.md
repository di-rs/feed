A software engineer pushes back on the two extreme reactions to AI — total replacement vs. "glorified autocomplete" — arguing both miss the point. AI predicts patterns at scale but lacks genuine understanding of systems, uptime concerns, team velocity, or long-term maintainability. The real shift isn't replacement but a fundamental reshaping of how engineers think and which human skills become most valuable.
