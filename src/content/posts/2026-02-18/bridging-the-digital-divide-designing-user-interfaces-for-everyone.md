Inclusive UI/UX design requires empathy for non-tech-savvy users — seniors, newcomers, and anyone who prefers simplicity — who are often left behind by visually spectacular but complex interfaces. These users rely on familiarity, need clear instructions, avoid risk, value consistency, and find technical jargon intimidating. The article advocates designing beyond the "modern" aesthetic trend toward interfaces that truly serve all people, not just the digitally fluent.
