Docker solves the classic "works on my machine" problem by packaging an app together with its runtime, libraries, and system dependencies into a portable container that runs identically everywhere. The guide introduces the key distinction between Images (immutable blueprints, like a class) and Containers (live running instances, like an object), building a strong mental model before diving into hands-on commands. It's a theory-first approach designed to make Docker click for absolute beginners before they touch the CLI.
