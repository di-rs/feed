The rise of generative AI has spawned an entirely new professional class — AI ethics officers, responsible AI leads, copyright liaisons, and digital provenance specialists — sitting at the confluence of technology, law, and creative practice. Chief AI Officer recruitment has tripled in five years, with over 40% of Fortune 500 companies expected to have the role by 2026, and the US government mandating federal agency CAIOs within 60 days in 2024. These roles require a rare blend of technical, legal, and ethical competencies that traditional hiring pipelines were never designed to produce.
