A test strategy answers the *why and what* of quality assurance — it's a philosophical, enduring document defining principles across projects — while a test plan answers the *how, when, and who* for a specific project with concrete dates and scope. Nearly two-thirds of QA teams confuse the two, resulting in misaligned stakeholders, duplicated effort, and documentation that can't scale. The article provides a definitive framework for creating both documents so they complement rather than conflict with each other.
