Framework choice has become largely irrelevant because all modern frameworks share the same underlying foundations — the same package registry, build tools, runtimes, and browsers — meaning failures ripple through everything regardless of which framework you picked. The author argues that modern frameworks have grown too large and interconnected to be meaningfully differentiated, and that teams should focus on the shared infrastructure layer rather than debating React vs. Vue vs. Svelte.
