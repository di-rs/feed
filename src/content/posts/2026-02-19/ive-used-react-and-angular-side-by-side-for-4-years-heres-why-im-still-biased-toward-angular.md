An engineer who spent four years working simultaneously on large Angular (~500 components) and React codebases makes the case that Angular's enforced structure — templates, services, guards, interceptors — scales more predictably than React's freedom, which accumulates architectural debt without strong team conventions. React gives flexibility, but Angular's opinionated patterns reduce decision fatigue and produce more maintainable codebases for larger teams over time.
